Quantum Mechanics

Interference

Quantum Waves Can Combine

Quantum particles can behave like waves. Sometimes waves get bigger OR cancel each other.

Quantum Interference

Why Interference Helps

Helping Correct Answers Stand Out

Constructive 🌊 + 🌊

Destructive 🌊 + upside-down 🌊

✗ Wrong answers disappear

Giant Number Challenge

Example number: 10,403

Classical Computer Method

One Guess at a Time

A classical computer checks 2, 3, 5, 7, 11… one-by-one.

For giant numbers, this can become VERY slow.

Quantum Computer Method

Quantum computers use superposition, interference, and entanglement to solve some giant problems faster.

Shor's Algorithm

A special quantum recipe that finds prime factors much faster than classical computers.

Shor's Algorithm is a step-by-step quantum procedure that uses superposition and interference to test many possible patterns in a number at once. You don't need the math details here — just say it lets a quantum computer discover prime factors way faster than trying every divisor one-by-one.
